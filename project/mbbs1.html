MBBS Admissions 2025: Everything You Need to Know

Welcome to your guide for MBBS admissions 2025. In this article, you will find everything you need to know about the NEET Entrance Exam, eligibility criteria, counselling process, and more.


NEET Entrance Exam for MBBS Admission

The majority of aspirants struggle to grasp how to get into a government college for MBBS. You should be aware that, the NEET Entrance Examination is the only choice for individuals seeking medical education in India. There is no other national level medical school entrance examination offered by any state or university. Thus, NEET is required for admission to either a government or a private college.


NEET Counselling 2025

NEET Counselling 2025 will be followed by MBBS Admission 2025. Candidates must take the NEET entrance exam in order to be admitted to the MBBS programme. The National Testing Agency (NTA) administers the NEET admission exam. However, there is no official announcement by the NTA about the NEET 2025 schedule. The NEET is the only National Level Entrance Exam that medical institutions in India accept in order to admit students to medical school.


NEET Exam 2025 Schedule

  • 1.NEET Exam Date: May 4, 2025
  • 2.NEET 2025 Registration Start Date: April 1, 2025
  • 3.NEET 2025 Notification: Last week of March 2025

Eligibility Criteria for NEET Entrance Examination 2025

To qualify for the NEET entrance examination, candidates must meet the following eligibility criteria:


  • 1.Indian Citizen (OCI candidates can also apply)
  • 2.Minimum age: 17 years as of 31st December of the year of admission
  • 3.Maximum age: 25 years (30 years for reserved categories)
  • 4.Pass in class 12 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English
  • 5.85% of MBBS/BDS seats are reserved for NEET state quota counselling. 15% of seats are allocated for All India Quota (AIQ).

NEET 2025 Application Process

To apply for the NEET 2025 exam, candidates must register online on the official NTA website. Registration fees for NEET 2025 are as follows:


  • 1.General Category: Rs. 1500
  • 2.General EWS/OBC NCL Category: Rs. 1400
  • 3.SC/ST/TWD/Transgender: Rs. 800

After registration, candidates will receive a NEET UG admit card. The exam will be conducted in paper-pencil mode, and results will be published after the exam.


NEET Counselling and Seat Allotment

The counselling process for MBBS admission involves online registration, selecting colleges, and seat allotment. Candidates will be allotted seats based on their NEET ranks. Document verification will be done at the allotted college. Below are the key documents required for seat allotment:


  • 1.10th and 12th Board Examination Admit Cards and Passing Certificates
  • 2.NEET Admit Card
  • 3.NEET Rank Card
  • 4.Category Certificate (for SC/ST/OBC candidates)
  • 5.Migration Certificate
  • 6.Passport Size Photograph

MBBS Course Fee

The MBBS course fee varies depending on the institution:


  • 1.Government Institutions: Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 50,000 per annum (central funded institutions)
  • 2.Private Medical Colleges: Rs. 10 lakhs to Rs. 50 lakhs for the entire course (management quota)

Duration of the MBBS Course

The MBBS course lasts for five and a half years, consisting of four and a half years of academic courses and a one-year compulsory rotating internship.


NEET UG 2025 Exam Process

The NEET UG exam will be held in May 2025. After the exam, the result will be declared, and candidates will be ranked based on their scores. The final admission will depend on the rank and preferences during the counselling process.
